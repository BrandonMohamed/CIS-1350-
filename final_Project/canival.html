Canival

What is carnival in Trinidad and Tobago? Trinidad and Tobago Carnival kicks off early Monday morning before sunrise, or late Sunday night, with J’Ouvert which is a sunrise parade. Monday and Tuesday of carnival are just two days of craziness. Trinidadians are party animals and will spend carnival drinking, dancing and socialising all day and all night long. Trinidad and Tobago Carnival is full of vibrant colourful parades and costumes which is the highlight of carnival.The origins of Trinidad and Tobago started in the 18th century by the French who had colonised the country.The French brought their cultural traditions and language to the island and one of the traditions were private parties and masquerade balls called fetes. The African slaves were forbidden from attending these celebrations so instead they held their own parties which were called canboulay. During their canboulay celebrations the slaves used to mock the behaviour and outfits of their masters which led to the creation of the ‘Mas’.
